03 / Lab · Side projects

What I built, what I learned.

02 case studies · Both live in production · Updated Apr 2026

Two production AI SaaS apps and the lessons I'm still digesting. Each one taught me something I couldn't have picked up from a tutorial — about LLMs, about scale, about the gap between "it works" and "it ships." The "What I learned" sections matter more than the stack lists.

01

Planitly.

planitly.com → · Launched 2024 · Live

AI-powered travel planning SaaS that generates personalized multi-day vacation itineraries using OpenAI GPT-4o, validated against TripAdvisor for real-world bookable activities.

Highlights03 / many
  • 01Two-phase AI generation: Zod-validated structured outputs, then TripAdvisor resolution in parallel.
  • 02Event-driven Inngest workflow — 11-step async job with retries and transactional commits.
  • 0316 Sequelize models, 74 migrations, 34 API endpoints. Complex relational schema, multi-provider auth.
Stack19 items
Next.js 14TypeScript MUI v6PostgreSQL InngestOpenAI GPT-4o Stripe +12
What I learned
  • Structured outputs (Zod schemas) changed everything. Free-text JSON is a debugging nightmare; schema-validated is night-and-day.
  • Background jobs are non-negotiable for LLM workflows. Anything > 5s needs Inngest, a queue, or you'll fight Vercel timeouts forever.
  • I over-engineered the database too early. Half those 16 models could've started as JSONB columns. Schema-shape your data after you know how it's queried.
02

Duebase.

duebase.com → · Launched 2025 · Live

AI-powered financial due diligence on UK Companies House filings. Risk scores and health assessments in 30 seconds using Gemini 2.5 Flash with structured JSON output.

Highlights03 / many
  • 01Gemini 2.5 Flash with structured JSON output — 40+ financial metrics extracted per filing.
  • 02Real-time search across 15M+ UK companies. Mapper pattern + parallel filing-history sync.
  • 03Daily payment reconciliation across Stripe and LemonSqueezy. Cron jobs that catch the things webhooks miss.
Stack20 items
Next.js 14TypeScript MUI v7Sequelize Gemini 2.5Inngest Stripe +13
What I learned
  • Gemini 2.5 Flash with structured outputs is shockingly good at financial document parsing — and an order of magnitude cheaper than GPT-4o for this workload.
  • Payment reconciliation is its own product. Two providers, three race conditions, one cron job. Worth getting right before you have paying users, not after.
  • Trial systems live or die by lifecycle logic. The 14-day reset cost me a week of bugs that only production traffic could've surfaced.
