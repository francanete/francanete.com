03 / Lab

Side projects.
What I built, what I learned.

Two production SaaS apps and the lessons I'm still digesting from shipping them. Each one taught me something I couldn't have learned from a tutorial — about LLMs, about scale, about the gap between "it works" and "it ships."

P

Planitly

Live
planitly.com · launched 2024

AI-powered travel planning SaaS that generates personalized multi-day vacation itineraries using OpenAI GPT-4o, validated against TripAdvisor.

Highlights
  • 01Two-phase AI generation with Zod-validated structured outputs
  • 02Event-driven Inngest workflow, 11-step background job
  • 0316 models, 74 migrations, 34 API endpoints
Stack
Next.js 14TypeScript MUI v6PostgreSQL InngestOpenAI GPT-4o Stripe+12
What I learned
  • Structured outputs (Zod schemas) changed everything. Free-text JSON is a debugging nightmare; schema-validated is night-and-day.
  • Background jobs are non-negotiable for LLM workflows. Anything that takes > 5s needs Inngest, queues, or you're going to fight Vercel timeouts forever.
  • I over-engineered the database too early. Half those 16 models could've started as JSONB columns. Lesson: schema-shape your data once you know how it's queried.
D

Duebase

Live
duebase.com · launched 2025

AI-powered financial due diligence on UK Companies House filings. Risk scores and health assessments in 30 seconds using Gemini 2.5 Flash.

Highlights
  • 01Gemini 2.5 Flash structured JSON, 40+ financial metrics extracted
  • 02Real-time search across 15M+ UK companies
  • 03Daily payment reconciliation across Stripe + LemonSqueezy
Stack
Next.js 14TypeScript MUI v7Sequelize Gemini 2.5Inngest Stripe+13
What I learned
  • Gemini 2.5 Flash with structured outputs is shockingly good at financial document parsing — and an order of magnitude cheaper than GPT-4o for this workload.
  • Payment reconciliation is its own product. Two providers, three race conditions, one cron job. Worth getting right early.
  • Trial systems live or die by lifecycle logic. The 14-day trial reset cost me a week of bugs that nothing but production traffic would've found.
